# ATLAS_VISUAL

**Visual law of the AstroLab universe.**
Companion to VOICE_FR.md. Governs every visual surface: site, Atlas, reading PDFs, Studio engine, social posts, future physical tier.

---

## I. The single principle

The world is **black, and light emerges from it.**
Never a colored ground. Never a flat fill of light. Every luminous form is made of many small marks — particles, filaments, hatching, cymatic ridges, scratched lines. Structure is always *revealed*, never *imposed*.

If you have to add light to make something work, the composition is wrong. Take light away until the darkness reveals it.

---

## II. Palette law

Four colors. Hard ceiling.

| Token | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Ink | `#0A0E1A` | The ground. Always. |
| Ivoire | `#F8F5EB` | Primary light. |
| Argent | `#C9C5B8` | Secondary light, depth, mid-tones. |
| Or | `#D9C98A` | Accent only. Sacred marking, instrument metal, sign emphasis. Never dominant. |

**Forbidden, without exception:** neon, purple cosmic clichés, cyan, electric blue, magenta, saturated reds, gradient skies. These break the world instantly.

The orange cymatic in the reference bank is a **structural** reference (pattern), not a palette reference. The medieval manuscript is a **lineage** reference (annotated cosmology), not a palette reference.

---

## III. Composition law

**Round.** The canvas may be rectangular but the world inside is always circular or radial. Sphere, disc, ring, mandala, lens, eye, dish, halo, orbit.

Implied geometry behind every composition: concentric rings, twelve-fold radial division (the houses), the cross of axes. Sometimes drawn, often only felt.

The center carries weight. The periphery breathes.

---

## IV. Texture law

Never flat fill. Light is **always** made of many marks. Five permitted textures:

- **Particles** — variable density, gravitational weighting
- **Filaments** — branching (neural, fungal, root, lightning)
- **Hatching** — fine cross-hatch (engraving lineage, ARCHÉ register)
- **Cymatic ridges** — sound figures, standing waves, vibrating membranes
- **Annotated marks** — scale ticks, coordinates, sigils, inscriptions

Test: if a fill looks *rendered* or *designed*, it's wrong. If it looks *observed* — microscope, oscilloscope, astrolabe, manuscript hand — it's right.

---

## V. Light behavior

Light **emerges from darkness** like phosphorescence. Three modes:

1. **Bloom** — bright at the dense center, fading to particulate edge
2. **Radiation** — filaments leaving the center, dimming outward
3. **Constellation** — sparse points accumulating until they form a recognizable shape

Forbidden: glow filters, lens flares, bloom shaders, neon strokes, drop shadows on luminous elements. The light comes from density of marks, not from post-processing.

---

## VI. Line quality

Observational, never decorative. Three registers, often combined:

- **Engraving** — fine cross-hatch, copperplate, manuscript plate (ARCHÉ lineage)
- **Scientific** — microscope phase contrast, oscilloscope traces, cymatic photography, particle simulation
- **Cartographic** — astrolabe inscriptions, celestial chart, calendar wheel, projection lines

Always annotated. Coordinates, ticks, sigils, hex codes (`#1A3A32`, `#FAF8F3`). The world is being **measured** even as it is beautiful. Beauty without measurement is decoration; measurement without beauty is data. AstroLab is both.

---

## VII. Motion law

- **Slow.** Adagio. The engine moves at the pace of a tide, not an animation.
- **Radial.** Outward bloom or inward settling. Never lateral pan, never camera shake, never cuts.
- **Breathing.** Density expands and contracts. The world inhales and exhales.
- **One held note at a time.** If two motions compete, the world becomes a music video. AstroLab is a manuscript.

Audio-reactivity (Studio engine) modulates **density, gold ratio, and constellation threshold** — never camera, never palette.

---

## VIII. Subject grammar

Three repeating subject classes, freely combined:

- **Cosmology** — instruments (astrolabe, armillary, sundial), charts (zodiac wheel, lunar phases, celestial map), projections, geometry
- **Biology** — neurons, colonies, growth, branching, feather, iris, hand. The body of the universe.
- **Physics** — cymatics, particles, fields, oscillation, currents. The voice of the universe.

A sign reading typically layers all three: instrument frame + biological pattern interior + cymatic vibration. The combination *is* the AstroLab signature.

The twelve signs are never rendered as cartoon animals. The lion does not roar. The ram does not leap. If symbols appear, they are engraved sigils within instruments, not characters within scenes.

## IX. The twelve as parameter sets

**Same engine. Twelve calibrations. Not twelve styles.**

| Sign | Behavior |
|---|---|
| Aries | Radial burst. Sparks. Sharp filaments. High gold accent. |
| Taurus | Slow concentric rings. Dense, low motion. Mineral. |
| Gemini | Paired filaments. Fast oscillation. Mirroring across the axis. |
| Cancer | Tidal expansion / contraction. Lunar emphasis. |
| Leo | Solar bloom. Radiating spokes. Gold dominant within the accent budget. |
| Virgo | Fine lattice. Crystalline density. Low motion. High precision. |
| Libra | Paired masses in equilibrium. Slow oscillation around axis. |
| Scorpio | Deep center. Recursive infall. Sparse outer field. |
| Sagittarius | Vector trajectory. Long filaments leaving the frame. |
| Capricorn | Slow vertical ascent. Structural. Few particles. |
| Aquarius | Network topology. Distributed nodes. Information flow. |
| Pisces | Vortex / currents. Dissolving boundaries. High flow rate. |

These are not visual styles. They are parameter calibrations of the **one** engine.

---

## X. What the world is NOT

- Not cyberpunk
- Not maximalist
- Not "magical" or "mystical" in the New Age sense
- Not animated like a film or a music video
- Not symbolic in the cartoon-zodiac sense (no leaping lions, no smiling moons)
- Not branded (no logos animating, no UI motion language)
- Not photographic (no skin, no products, no UGC)

The world is **observatory**, not entertainment. Reverent, slow, measured. A manuscript made of light.

---

## XII. The single decision rule

Before shipping any visual, ask one question:

**Does it belong to the same museum as the rest?**

If the answer requires explanation, the answer is no.
